# Writing Rules

## Required

- Never use em dashes.
- Never replace em dashes with en dashes, double hyphens, or spaced hyphens.
- Never restructure with punctuation that imitates an em dash pause.
- Rewrite the sentence so no dash-like pause is needed.

## Avoid

- Generic openings like "I hope this message finds you well."
- Generic closings like "Please do not hesitate to reach out."
- Overly polished, overly formal, or impersonal wording.
- Corporate filler, buzzwords, and vague business jargon.
- Repetitive transition words like "moreover," "furthermore," and "additionally."
- Boilerplate reassurance, politeness, or praise.
- Empty qualifiers like "valuable," "seamless," "robust," or "tailored" unless specific and necessary.
- Long introductions before the actual point.
- Obvious summaries that repeat what was already said.
- Symmetrical, formulaic paragraph structure.
- Lists when a short plain sentence would do.
- Generic wording that could apply to any client, project, or situation.
- Repeated sentence patterns or repeated phrasing.
- Overexplaining simple points.
- Fake warmth, fake urgency, or fake certainty.
- Claims of personal experience, opinions, or actions that did not happen.
- Unnecessary adjectives and adverbs.
- Passive phrasing when a direct sentence is clearer.
- Words or phrases that sound written for a template instead of a real person.
